FREE LANDSCAPING INVOICE TEMPLATE AUSTRALIA — ABN & GST READY

Landscaping jobs involve a unique mix of labour, materials, equipment hire, and sometimes subcontractors. Your invoice needs to reflect all of this clearly — both for your client's understanding and for ATO compliance.

Whether you're doing a full garden renovation or a weekly mow-and-blow, here's exactly what your landscaping invoice should include, common pricing mistakes, and a ready-to-use example.

WHAT BELONGS ON A LANDSCAPING INVOICE

Landscaping invoices need to be more detailed than most trades because you're often supplying both labour and significant materials. Here's what to include:

  • Your business name, ABN, and contact details
  • Client's name and property address — especially important if the billing address differs from the job site
  • Invoice number and date
  • Detailed description of work — "Garden maintenance" is too vague. List specific tasks: mowing, edging, hedge trimming, mulching, planting
  • Materials supplied — itemise plants, mulch, soil, pavers, sleepers, etc. with quantities and unit prices
  • Equipment hire — if you hired a mini excavator or dingo, list it
  • Labour hours and rate — or a flat project rate
  • Waste removal fees — tip runs, skip bins, green waste disposal
  • GST breakdown (if registered)
  • Payment terms and bank details

HOURLY VS FLAT-RATE PRICING

Most landscapers use one of two models:

Hourly rate — common for maintenance work (mowing, weeding, hedge trimming). Typical rates range from $50-$85/hr for sole traders. Show the hours and rate on your invoice so the client can see exactly what they're paying for.

Flat project rate — better for larger jobs like retaining walls, paving, or full garden makeovers. Quote the whole job as one price, but still break down materials separately. This protects your margin and avoids arguments about how long something took.

Seasonal pricing tip: Many landscapers charge higher rates in spring and summer when demand peaks. If you adjust your rates seasonally, make sure your invoices always reflect the current rate — and consider locking in regular clients at a consistent rate to retain them year-round.

ATO REQUIREMENTS FOR LANDSCAPERS

The same ATO rules apply to landscapers as any other trade:

  • ABN is mandatory — without it, clients withhold 47% of your payment
  • GST registration is required if your annual turnover exceeds $75,000
  • Tax invoices over $1,000 must include the buyer's identity or ABN
  • Keep copies of all invoices for at least 5 years
  • Record all material purchases — you can claim GST credits on business expenses if GST-registered

If you're doing work for a builder or property manager, they'll often require your ABN before they'll even engage you. Having a clean, professional invoice template makes this process seamless.

COMMON MISTAKES ON LANDSCAPING INVOICES

1. NOT ITEMISING MATERIALS

Just writing "materials — $900" invites questions. List what you supplied: 6 cubic metres of mulch, 24 plants, 28m of edging. Clients respect transparency and it reduces payment disputes.

2. FORGETTING WASTE DISPOSAL

Tip fees, trailer loads, skip bin hire — these add up fast on landscaping jobs. If you don't list them on the invoice, you're eating those costs. Always include green waste disposal as a line item.

3. LUMPING EVERYTHING INTO ONE LINE

A single line item of "$2,400 — landscaping" looks unprofessional and makes it impossible to track your own margins. Break it into labour, materials, and other costs. Your future self will thank you when it's tax time.

4. NOT SPECIFYING THE JOB SITE ADDRESS

Especially for property managers or landlords, the billing address and job site are often different. Always include both to avoid confusion.
