THEBRICKBOOK VS SERVICEM8: WHICH IS BETTER FOR AUSTRALIAN TRADIES?

ServiceM8 is one of the most popular job management apps in Australia. It's built by an Australian team, used by thousands of trade businesses, and has a reputation for excellent client communication features. If you've seen a tradie send you an "on the way" text with a photo and arrival time — they're probably using ServiceM8.

So how does it compare to TheBrickBook? The answer depends on what problem you're trying to solve.

WHAT IS SERVICEM8?

ServiceM8 is a field service management app designed for trade businesses. It's particularly strong in managing the client experience — automated booking confirmations, "on my way" notifications, before-and-after job photos, on-site quoting, and digital signatures. It also handles job dispatch, scheduling, invoicing, and integrates with Xero and MYOB.

Pricing starts at $29/month ($348/year) for the Starter plan, scaling up to $349/month for larger businesses. Most solo operators would use the $29 or $49 plan.

WHAT IS THEBRICKBOOK?

TheBrickBook is a free iOS app for Australian tradies and solo operators. It focuses on a different problem: profit clarity. It tracks jobs, logs expenses per job, has a built-in timer, creates invoices with ABN and GST, and calculates your real hourly rate automatically. No monthly fee, no limits.

WHERE SERVICEM8 WINS

ServiceM8 has earned its reputation. Here's what it does better than almost anything else:

  • Client communication: Automated booking confirmations, "on my way" notifications with live ETA, follow-up emails after jobs. Your clients feel like they're dealing with a large, professional operation — even if it's just you.
  • Before-and-after photos: Attach photos to each job for your records and to show clients the completed work. Great for compliance and disputes.
  • Online booking: Clients can book jobs through a portal that syncs to your calendar. Less phone tag.
  • On-site quoting: Build and send quotes on the spot, then convert to a job when accepted.
  • Digital signatures: Get client sign-off on site. Useful for compliance-heavy trades.
  • Australian-built: Both apps are Australian, but ServiceM8 has been around longer and has deep integrations with Australian tools like Xero and MYOB.

WHERE THEBRICKBOOK WINS

  • Price: Free vs $348+/year. TheBrickBook has no monthly fee, no tiered plans, no feature gating.
  • Profit visibility: ServiceM8 tells you what you invoiced. TheBrickBook tells you what you kept — your actual profit per job, your real hourly rate after all expenses.
  • Per-job expense tracking: Log every expense against the specific job it belongs to. See exactly how much each job cost you and what you earned.
  • Simplicity: ServiceM8 is powerful but has a learning curve. It takes time to set up templates, automations, and integrations. TheBrickBook works out of the box.
  • Focus: TheBrickBook does one thing extremely well — it shows you what you actually earn. No feature bloat, no settings to configure.

DIFFERENT TOOLS FOR DIFFERENT PROBLEMS

ServiceM8 solves the client experience problem: "How do I look professional, communicate well, and manage jobs efficiently?"

TheBrickBook solves the profit clarity problem: "How much am I actually making on each job, and what's my real hourly rate?"

These are genuinely different problems. A tradie can have excellent client communication and still not know what they actually earn per hour. Conversely, you can know your exact profit on every job but still send clients plain-text invoices with no booking system.

The question is: which problem is more important to you right now?

WHEN SERVICEM8 IS THE RIGHT CHOICE

  • You want to impress clients with professional automated communications
  • You need online booking and scheduling
  • Before-and-after photos are important for your trade (pest control, cleaning, painting)
  • You want everything integrated with Xero or MYOB
  • You're willing to invest time in setup for long-term workflow gains

WHEN THEBRICKBOOK IS THE RIGHT CHOICE

  • You want to know what you actually earn — per job, per hour
  • You're a solo operator who doesn't need client automation
  • You want something that works immediately, no setup required
  • You'd rather not spend $348+/year on software
  • You care more about profit visibility than client communication tools

OUR RECOMMENDATION

If client communication and professional presentation are your top priority, ServiceM8 is an excellent choice. It's Australian, it's proven, and the client experience features are best-in-class.

But if you're a solo tradie who wants to understand your profit — what you actually take home after expenses on every single job — TheBrickBook answers that question for free. No setup, no monthly fee, no complexity. Just clarity on what you earn.
